  • Maintain tasting rooms and retail operations
  • Carry full G&A and operational overhead
  • Result: Break-even or losses even with good brands

BSC Model - Portland Craft, Lean Operations

  • Leverage Park Street Marketplace for distribution

  • Result: Immediate profitability, scalable growth

The Distribution Challenge: Winning the Bartender

Core Insight: Bartenders are the ultimate gatekeepers. They control what gets poured, recommended, and talked about. Distribution gets you on the shelf—bartender advocacy gets you in the glass.

Traditional Approach Fails

  • Generic sales pitches
  • Price-focused selling
  • No emotional connection
  • Competing against big budgets

BSC Story-First Model

  • Compelling brand narratives
  • Bartender education & empowerment
  • Community building
  • Grassroots advocacy

Every Brand Needs a Story Bartenders Can Tell

Brand Narrative Framework

  • Origin Story: Why was this brand created? What's the founder's journey?
  • Craft Credentials: What makes the liquid special? Unique aging, ingredients, process
  • Local/Regional Roots: Portland heritage, Pacific Northwest ingredients, community ties
  • Underdog Narrative: Rescued brands, second chances, David vs. Goliath positioning
Example: "Azunia Black - A 2-year añejo that proves patience beats marketing budgets. This is what tequila tastes like when you don't rush it."
